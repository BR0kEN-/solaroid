# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solaroid is used by solar plant owners and operators who monitor production, inverter-reported losses, import/export balance, ROI, payback, forecasts, and plant-to-plant comparisons. The primary context is a Home Assistant dashboard, often on iPhone-sized screens, where users check status quickly and repeatedly rather than explore a marketing story.

## Product Purpose

Solaroid turns Home Assistant solar and grid telemetry into a trustworthy financial and energy dashboard backed by Supabase. Success means the user can understand current performance, compare plants or periods, verify payment logic, and see investment recovery without needing to inspect spreadsheets or raw sensor data.

## Brand Personality

Calm precise utility. The interface should feel restrained, reliable, and data-literate: clear enough for daily household monitoring, precise enough for tariff, tax, currency, and ROI decisions, and quiet enough to sit naturally inside Home Assistant.

## Anti-references

Solaroid should not look like a SaaS landing page, a decorative consumer energy app, or a novelty chart demo. Avoid over-branded surfaces, gratuitous illustrations, ornamental motion, loud gradients, and chart systems that make the data harder to compare. Do not trade mobile readability or Home Assistant fit for visual flourish.

## Design Principles

- Preserve trust in the numbers: calculations, labels, comparison states, and measurement boundaries should make the underlying energy and financial logic inspectable.
- Optimize for repeated monitoring: controls should be predictable, touch-friendly, and quick to scan on mobile.
- Keep density disciplined: show rich operational data without turning every metric into a decorative card or semantic color signal.
- Fit Home Assistant first: transparent or neutral backgrounds, light/dark support, and iPhone-sized layouts are part of the product environment.
- Reuse established patterns: charts, inspectors, segmented controls, tables, and modals should extend the existing vocabulary before inventing a new one.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as a practical baseline for contrast, focus states, keyboard access, and readable text. Prioritize mobile touch targets, reduced-motion safety, light and dark color schemes, and robust English/Ukrainian labels. Keep Ukrainian energy terminology consistent, including `ПІ` for ROI and `кВт·г` for kWh.
